# Vision Overview

The online campus is not only a broadcast destination. It is a real ministry environment where people can be known, discipled, cared for, and invited into next steps with Jesus and the church.

## Core questions

This section exists to answer:

- Why does the online campus exist?
- Who is it for?
- What kind of community are we trying to build?
- What outcomes matter most?
- What should never be mistaken for success?

## Working purpose statement

The online campus exists to help people meaningfully engage with the church from wherever they are, while creating clear pathways toward spiritual formation, personal connection, and next steps in community.

## Desired outcomes

The online campus should aim to produce:

- Consistent weekly engagement with worship and teaching
- Real-time hospitality through chat and host presence
- Clear pathways into prayer, baptism, membership, serving, and groups
- Pastoral awareness of people who need care or follow-up
- Stories of life change, not just audience size

## Risks to watch

The online campus can drift if it becomes:

- A content channel instead of a community
- A production burden with no pastoral payoff
- Dependent on one person holding too much knowledge
- Focused only on views instead of people taking next steps
