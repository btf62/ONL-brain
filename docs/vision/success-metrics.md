# Success Metrics

These metrics are intended to keep the online campus focused on ministry health, not just audience volume.

## Category 1: Reach

Useful for understanding visibility, but not enough by themselves.

- Live views
- On-demand views
- Unique viewers
- Average watch time
- Engagement rate in chat

## Category 2: Connection

These reveal whether people are moving from anonymous attendance toward known community.

- First-time online guests identified
- New chat participants
- Direct conversations started
- Prayer requests submitted
- Connection forms completed

## Category 3: Next steps

These show whether the ministry is helping people move toward discipleship and belonging.

- Baptism interest submissions
- Membership interest submissions
- Serving interest submissions
- Group interest submissions
- Follow-up conversations completed

## Category 4: Care

These show whether people are being shepherded well.

- Prayer requests responded to
- Care follow-ups completed
- Crisis escalations handled within target time
- People with no response after outreach attempts

## Category 5: Operations health

These help prevent hidden strain and recurring execution failures.

- Weekend service readiness completed on time
- Script deadlines met
- Streaming incidents
- Serve team coverage by role
- Post-service follow-up completed within target window

## Suggested scorecard cadence

- Weekly: operations, engagement, follow-up completion
- Monthly: trends, serve team health, next-step movement
- Quarterly: strategy review and success-metric refinement

## Notes

Metrics should support stories, not replace them. Every reporting rhythm should include both numbers and a short narrative summary of what God appears to be doing in the online community.
