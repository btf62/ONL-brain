# Online Campus Definition

This is a working definition for the online campus.

The online campus is a real ministry environment for people who engage with Northridge through digital means and need pastoral care, connection, discipleship, and clear next steps.

It is not only:

- a livestream
- a content archive
- a Sunday morning technical workflow
- a backup option for people who cannot attend in person

It is also:

- a front door for people exploring Northridge
- a pastoral environment for people who participate online regularly
- a next-step pathway for prayer, baptism, membership, serving, and groups
- a weekly worship and teaching environment
- a system of follow-up, care, and response

## Experience promise

The online campus should aim to offer:

- a reliable way to worship with Northridge online
- clear pathways to ask for prayer or take a next step
- timely pastoral response when someone reaches out
- human presence through chat, text, email, and follow-up
- continuity with the broader church while acknowledging online-specific realities

## Open definition questions

- Who is the primary audience: existing Northridge attenders, people exploring Northridge, geographically distant attenders, shut-ins, or a broader mission field?
- What experience can we responsibly promise every online participant?
- What should be handled centrally, and what should be handled by the online campus pastor or serve teams?
- What level of personal follow-up is realistic at current staffing?
- What parts of online ministry should be delegated, automated, or stopped?
