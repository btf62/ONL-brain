# Connection Pipelines

This document outlines the major response pathways that should be tracked and maintained in Rock RMS or associated ministry systems.

## Primary pipelines

### Prayer requests

- Capture request
- Assess urgency
- Respond pastorally
- Assign follow-up when needed
- Close loop after response

### Baptism interest

- Capture interest
- Verify contact information
- Assign follow-up conversation
- Track conversation completion
- Move person to next instruction or event

### Membership interest

- Capture interest
- Send next-step information
- Invite to class or process
- Track attendance and completion

### Serving interest

- Identify ministry area of interest
- Route to appropriate leader
- Track contact and placement
- Confirm onboarding completion

### Group or community interest

- Identify preferred type of connection
- Route to available next step
- Track response and placement

## Pipeline standards

Every pipeline should define:

- Intake source
- Owner
- First-response expectation
- Follow-up expectation
- Completion definition
- Reporting metric

## Rock RMS documentation prompts

For each pathway, document:

- Which workflow or connection opportunity is used
- Which statuses matter
- Which team owns each handoff
- Which fields must be filled out
- What triggers overdue follow-up

## Risk areas

- Duplicate records or incomplete contact info
- Requests with no assigned owner
- Delayed response to urgent care needs
- Inconsistent use of statuses across team members
