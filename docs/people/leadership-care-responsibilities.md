# Leadership Care Responsibilities

This document captures two pastoral leadership-care responsibilities that are part of Brad's broader campus pastor work.

These responsibilities are adjacent to online campus work, but they matter because they compete for the same pastoral attention, calendar capacity, and follow-up energy.

## Key leader care

Canonical system:

- Rock RMS key leader dashboard

Responsibility:

- Maintain healthy touchpoints with assigned key leaders throughout the year.

Touchpoint types may include:

- text
- phone call
- email
- campus hallway conversation
- visit
- coffee or meal
- personal encouragement

What good looks like:

- assigned key leaders are not invisible between major ministry moments
- touchpoints are spread across the year instead of clustered around urgency
- Rock reflects enough activity to show whether key leaders are being cared for
- the pastor can quickly see who has not been contacted recently

Repo role:

- define the responsibility
- identify dashboard questions
- capture coaching rhythms and planning prompts
- avoid duplicating the full Rock dashboard

## Community group coach responsibility

Canonical system:

- Rock RMS Community Groups / Group Viewer

Responsibility:

- Serve as a small groups coach for assigned community group leaders.
- Maintain relational connection, encouragement, and accountability with those leaders.

Known recent touchpoint:

- Around Christmas, Brad gave each assigned community group leader a book, personal thank-you card, and gift card.

Likely ongoing touchpoint types:

- texts
- check-in calls
- informal Sunday conversations
- coffee or meals
- encouragement notes
- seasonal gifts or thank-you cards
- coaching conversations when a group is struggling

What good looks like:

- group leaders know they are seen and appreciated
- each assigned leader receives regular touchpoints
- leaders have a clear path to ask questions or surface issues
- stale relationships are visible before they become discouragement or drift

## Dashboard questions

A leadership-care dashboard should answer:

- Who am I responsible for?
- When was the last meaningful touchpoint?
- What type of touchpoint was it?
- Who is overdue for care?
- Which leaders need more than a lightweight check-in?
- Which touchpoints should happen this week?
- Which responsibilities live in Rock, and which belong on my personal closed list?

## Suggested cadence

Daily:

- no default daily requirement; avoid turning this into background guilt

Weekly:

- choose one to three leadership-care touchpoints for the week
- log or verify completed touchpoints in the canonical system when appropriate

Monthly:

- review key leaders and community group leaders for stale contact
- identify leaders needing a deeper conversation

Seasonal:

- plan appreciation, encouragement, or gifts
- review whether the assigned leader list is still accurate
