# Care Escalation

This document defines how higher-risk or more sensitive pastoral situations should be recognized and handed off.

## Situations that may require escalation

- Self-harm or harm-to-others language
- Abuse disclosure
- Acute crisis or mental health emergency
- Immediate safety concerns
- Repeated high-needs interaction beyond serve team member scope

## Escalation principles

- Serve team members should never carry crisis care alone
- Urgent situations should move quickly to appropriate pastoral leadership
- Documentation should be accurate, minimal, and timely
- Privacy should be respected while still enabling action

## Basic escalation flow

1. Recognize the concern.
2. Move the conversation to the appropriate channel if possible.
3. Alert the designated pastor or staff owner immediately.
4. Document the interaction and handoff in the agreed system.
5. Confirm the handoff was received.

## Training requirement

Any serve team member serving in chat, prayer, or host roles should know:

- What they can respond to directly
- What they should escalate
- Who to contact
- How fast to act

This document should eventually be expanded with ministry-specific guidance approved by church leadership.
