# ONL Mindmup Summary

Source file: [ONL-Mindmup.pdf](ONL-Mindmup.pdf)

## What this is

This is a one-page mind map that captures an earlier framing of the online campus role and ecosystem.

It is valuable because it shows:

- the major work categories already living in the role
- the range of systems and platforms involved
- the overlap between pastoral, operational, technical, and strategic work
- early ideas for potential owners or supporting people

## What it tells us about the online campus

The online campus was already being imagined as more than a livestream. The map treats it as a ministry system with four broad dimensions:

- vision and digital ministry strategy
- operations and production
- people, pastoring, and connections
- platforms, systems, and technical maintenance

That aligns strongly with the repo direction we have started building.

## Key themes from the map

### 1. Vision and strategy were already a major responsibility

The map includes:

- define digital ministry vision
- goals and strategy
- identify mission field
- develop and implement digital ministry initiatives
- metrics
- brain trust of advisors

This suggests the online campus pastor role was never only executional. Strategy and ministry definition are central responsibilities.

### 2. Operations work is broad and production-heavy

The map includes:

- weekly streaming
- scriptwriting
- video production and editing
- full-service post edits for YouTube or cable
- studio design
- podcast
- scheduling and training

This reinforces that operations should remain a major top-level section in the repo, with special attention to recurring production workflows and handoffs.

### 3. Technical systems are a meaningful domain of work

The map includes:

- acquisition, configuration, troubleshooting, automation, and documentation
- console equipment
- streaming studio
- sysadmin
- platform maintenance
- Church Online Platform
- SubSplash
- YouTube Live
- Facebook Live
- Rock RMS
- Rock API

This tells us the online campus role has a real technical operations layer, not just ministry tasks. That likely deserves deeper system documentation over time.

### 4. Pastoring and connection pathways are central

The map includes:

- prayer
- counseling
- chat hosting
- connections
- connection flows and scoring system for campus pastors
- staff training
- community groups
- lead in-person group
- lead online group

This validates the `people` section of the repo and suggests future documentation should spell out how pastoral response, prayer, group connection, and follow-up actually work.

### 5. The role is carrying too many hats

The map mixes:

- visionary leadership
- product ownership
- technical systems work
- content and script work
- pastoral care
- platform administration
- group leadership
- reporting and reusable components

That confirms the role-map work in this repo is necessary. The mind map shows a highly overloaded role rather than a narrowly scoped job.

### 6. Delegation opportunities were already visible

The map repeatedly names potential resources or likely support roles, including areas like:

- writing
- video production
- prayer
- counseling
- chat hosting
- online groups
- technical and sysadmin support
- Rock RMS and development support

This is a strong sign that the repo should keep growing toward documented serve team roles, not just personal task lists.

## Notable organizational buckets from the map

These are the clearest functional groupings visible in the source material:

- Vision
- Operations
- Pastoring
- ONL Tech Team
- ONL Campus Pastor
- Target groups
- Platforms and systems
- Potential resource owners

## Notable target groups named in the map

The map specifically calls out:

- parolees and sex offenders
- shut-ins
- geographically distant attenders
- top givers

This suggests the ministry was also thinking in terms of distinct pastoral segments, not just a generic online audience.
