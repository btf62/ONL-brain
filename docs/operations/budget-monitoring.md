# Budget Monitoring

This document captures the online campus pastor's budget-awareness responsibilities.

It is a starter page until actual budget lines, owners, and reporting sources are added.

## Purpose

Budget monitoring exists to help the online campus pastor know:

- which budgets are assigned to the role
- how much has been spent
- what is committed but not yet spent
- where spending is likely to run out
- which upcoming ministry plans need budget review before action

## Current source inputs

Known or likely inputs:

- weekly financial reports
- assigned budget lines
- purchase requests or reimbursements
- online campus production costs
- giveaway costs
- technology, equipment, and repair costs
- subscriptions or platform costs
- event or initiative-specific expenses

## Questions to answer

For each budget:

- What is the budget name or account?
- Who owns it?
- What ministry work does it support?
- What is the annual budget?
- How much has been spent to date?
- What spending is already planned or committed?
- What is the remaining usable balance?
- What deadline or season affects spending?
- What needs attention this week?

## Dashboard candidates

- budget line summary
- percent spent by budget
- remaining balance by budget
- projected year-end position
- upcoming known expenses
- risk flags for budgets likely to run short

## Operating rhythm

Weekly:

- review the latest financial report
- identify budget lines that changed materially
- flag unexpected charges or missing expenses
- note upcoming purchases or events

Monthly:

- compare actual spending to expected pace
- review upcoming ministry plans against available budget
- decide whether spending needs to slow, shift, or be requested

Quarterly:

- review budget health with the appropriate supervisor or finance contact
- identify structural underfunding or repeated surprises
- document lessons for next year's budget planning

## Open setup work

- collect the list of budgets currently assigned to the online campus pastor
- identify where weekly financial reports come from
- decide whether budget tracking belongs in a spreadsheet, dashboard, or simple CSV
- add fields to the dashboard backlog once the reporting format is clear
