# Online Campus Playbook Outline

This is a placeholder outline for a future online campus playbook.

The playbook should become the readable operating guide for how the online campus works. It should be based on repo docs, official Northridge playbooks, current source documents, and actual weekly practice.

## Proposed sections

### 1. Purpose and scope

- what the online campus is
- who it serves
- what success looks like
- what the online campus does not own

### 2. Weekly service rhythm

- early-week planning
- scriptwriting and talking points
- service readiness
- chat host readiness
- production readiness
- post-service follow-through

### 3. Online front door

- `iwant.info`
- live chat
- `TEXT HELLO`
- prayer request forms
- email notifications
- baptism, membership, serving, and group interest forms

### 4. Response standards

- prayer response
- baptism response
- membership response
- serving response
- group/community response
- follow-up pursuit rhythm
- escalation paths

### 5. Serve teams

- chat host team
- production or tech team
- admin support team
- team leads
- onboarding and training
- scheduling and coverage expectations

### 6. Technical systems

- streaming rack
- Church Online Platform or current live platform
- ProPresenter
- PCO
- Subsplash or app readiness
- Rock RMS

### 7. Scripts and media

- weekly talking points
- host moments
- closing comments
- video production
- relationship to ScriptBrain

### 8. Metrics, reports, and budgets

- weekly scorecard
- response-time dashboard
- Text HELLO count
- prayer and next-step metrics
- financial reports
- budget monitoring

### 9. Known gaps

- source systems that still need review
- stale Rock roster cleanup
- undocumented email and form queues
- unclear central versus campus follow-up ownership
- admin support role vacancy

## Playbook rule

The playbook should be readable enough for staff and serve team leaders, while the repo remains the structured source of truth behind it.
